# Ashok Leyland – 3PL Vendor Performance Study

## Overview

Conducted a third-party logistics (3PL) vendor performance study during my internship at Ashok Leyland to identify operational bottlenecks, coordination challenges, and opportunities to improve outbound logistics efficiency across multiple locations.

## Objective

The study aimed to evaluate vendor performance, understand key operational challenges, and identify opportunities to strengthen coordination across the outbound logistics network.

## Methodology

- Gathered and analysed feedback from 50+ logistics stakeholders across operational locations.
- Assessed transportation workflows, communication practices, and delivery coordination processes.
- Evaluated recurring operational challenges affecting delivery timelines and vendor performance.
- Reviewed current practices related to driver management, scheduling, documentation, and emergency dispatches.

## Key Findings

- Driver management and rest planning emerged as critical operational concerns.
- Communication gaps and reliance on informal coordination channels affected operational visibility.
- Schedule planning and adherence presented opportunities for improvement.
- Documentation processes required greater standardisation and SOP compliance.
- Enhanced digital tracking could improve coordination across stakeholders.

## Recommendations

- Strengthen digital coordination mechanisms across logistics stakeholders.
- Improve route planning and driver management practices.
- Standardise documentation and operational workflows.
- Introduce vendor performance scorecards and tracking mechanisms.
- Increase visibility through operational analytics and reporting.

## Outcome

Developed actionable recommendations to improve transporter performance, strengthen operational coordination, and support more efficient outbound logistics operations.
